Lecture: Medication Adherence and Why It Matters

- Definition:
  Medication adherence refers to whether patients take their medicines as prescribed — not missing doses, stopping early, or taking incorrect amounts.

- Why It’s Important:
  1. Better disease control.  
  2. Reduces the risk of hospitalization.  
  3. Lowers healthcare costs in the long run.

- Strategies to Improve Adherence:
  - Use reminders (digital tools, apps).  
  - Link medication time with daily routines (e.g., after brushing teeth).  
  - Educate patients about their illness and how the drug works.  
  - Use pill boxes or packaging that organizes daily doses.

- Challenges:
  - Forgetfulness  
  - Side effects  
  - Cost of medication  
  - Lack of understanding

- The Role of Technology:
  Tools like ThummimAI can help by reminding users, tracking logs, and giving motivational messages.



  Lecture: Understanding Vitamin D.

- What is Vitamin D?
  Vitamin D is a fat‑soluble vitamin that helps regulate calcium and phosphate in the body.

- Why is it important?
  It supports bone health, immune function, and has roles in reducing inflammation.

- Sources:
  1. Sunlight – UVB triggers vitamin D production on your skin.
  2. Food – Fatty fish, fortified milk, and eggs.
  3. Supplements – Often used when dietary intake or sunlight exposure is low.

- Recommended Daily Dose:
  Adults: 600 – 800 IU (depending on age and health status)
  
- Interactions:
  Can interact with medications like corticosteroids and weight-loss drugs.

- Tips:
  - Take with food for better absorption.
  - Monitor with blood tests if used long-term.
